What I am Building

GYMOS – AI-Powered Fitness Tracking and Recommendation Platform

GYMOS is a full-stack fitness product I ship to my own phone first: it records training sessions, nutrition, and trajectory over weeks—not a demo timeline. The app turns that stream into plans people can execute, then revises those plans when volume, recovery, or macros drift so users are not stuck maintaining a spreadsheet of guesses.

  • AI-driven workout and meal suggestions tied to stated goals, recent sessions, and adherence—not generic templates.
  • Live tracking for sets, meals, and markers of progress so the dashboard reflects what happened today, not last Sunday’s export.
  • Adaptive scheduling that shortens, swaps, or reloads blocks when performance signals missed work, overload, or plateau.
  • Profiles, targets, and guardrails kept in one place so recommendations stay personal as data grows.
  • Analytics views for load trends, consistency, and nutrition gaps—built for quick decisions on a small screen.
  • Interface tuned for thumb reach, fast logging, and glanceable summaries on real devices in real gyms.

Problem

Logging apps collect data but rarely close the loop. Users still rebuild weekly plans by hand, which breaks the moment travel, injury, or diet slip happens.

Solution

GYMOS unifies capture, analytics, and recommendation behind Flask APIs and a React client. Data-driven rules score recent behavior so the next training and nutrition block stays aligned with what the user can sustain.

Impact

One product surface replaces fragmented trackers, notes, and calculators—so progress, streaks, and weak spots stay visible without hand-maintaining multiple tools.

Backend services own user data, recommendation passes, and dashboard aggregates; the architecture leaves clear seams for deeper AI coaching without rewriting core flows.
